WHY WE EXIST

ONE BODY. TWELVE MONTHS. TOTAL WAR.

Her Body begins with one young woman living with severe cerebral palsy — our Patient Zero. Cerebral palsy is not one disease. It is dozens of problems occurring together: swallowing, muscle tone, nutrition, orthopedic deformity, communication, posture, pain, sleep, isolation. Medicine treats them one clinic visit at a time. We treat them like a campaign — every subsystem gets its own front, its own specialists, its own metrics, and its own deadline.

We are honest about the limit: no one can reverse the brain injury underneath cerebral palsy. So we don't define victory as a cure. We define it as function — eating safely, communicating clearly, operating technology independently, moving without pain. We push her biology to its ceiling. And where her body no longer listens to her, we engineer a body that does.

FRONT I · BIOLOGY — MONTHS 01–12

PUSH THE BODY TO ITS LIMIT

A world-class multidisciplinary clinical program run with startup discipline. Six workstreams attack six subsystems in parallel:

  • Assess — a 500-page baseline across 14+ specialties. No treatment before total intelligence.
  • Swallow — instrumented studies (VFSS/FEES), then therapy. Safe eating is objective #1.
  • Hands — not "normal hands." Useful hands: hold a spoon, press a switch, drive a chair.
  • Feet — orthopedic correction for positioning, transfers, and pain — measured, not promised.
  • Confidence — nutrition, posture, style, travel, purpose. Capability grows confidence.
  • Twin — every result digitized into her Digital Twin from day one.
FRONT II · MACHINE — MONTHS 03 → YEARS 2–5

ENGINEER WHAT BIOLOGY CAN'T

The clinical campaign generates something no lab has: a continuous, consented, whole-person dataset of one human being fighting for function. That dataset becomes:

  • An AI caregiver that coordinates specialists and never forgets a detail.
  • An escalation ladder to robotics — voice, vision, home robot, mobile manipulation, and finally humanoid assistance. Hardware arrives when the software has earned it.

FRONT II · THE HUMAN DIGITAL TWIN

ONE PATIENT. ONE MODEL. EVERY DISEASE EVENTUALLY.

We are not building "AI for cerebral palsy." We are building the Human Digital Twin — a continuously updated model of one whole person: medical history, medications, therapy response, range of motion, swallowing progress, speech, facial expression, nutrition, sleep, emotional state, goals, and daily routines. It coordinates her care, personalizes her therapy, and preserves knowledge across every clinician and caregiver she will ever have. Then it scales — to stroke, spinal cord injury, Parkinson's, MS, TBI, and age-related disability.

LAYER 1 · SENSE — Whole-person data capture. Motion capture, 3D gait and posture scans, instrumented swallow studies, wearables, therapy session logs, imaging, bloodwork — one schema, one timeline, one patient.
LAYER 2 · MODEL — The Twin itself. A living state model that tracks every subsystem, predicts therapy response, flags regressions early, and answers the question every specialist asks: "what has changed since I last saw her?"
LAYER 3 · ACT — Agents and machines. The AI caregiver schedules, reminds, and briefs. The voice and vision systems give her communication and control. The robots — when they arrive — inherit a model that already knows her body better than any manual ever could.
